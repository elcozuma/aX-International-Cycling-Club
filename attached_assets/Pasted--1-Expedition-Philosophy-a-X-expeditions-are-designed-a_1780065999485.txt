
1. Expedition Philosophy
a-X expeditions are designed around shared adventure, autonomy and small-group expedition culture.
This is not a luxury guided tour or fully supported package holiday.
Participants are encouraged to:
• Make independent decisions;
• Ride at their own pace;
• Support one another where possible;
• Embrace uncertainty and adventure responsibly.

2. Event Overview
Event: a-X Anti-Atlas Group Expedition - 21 March to 28 March 2027
Region: Southern Morocco / Anti-Atlas
Estimated Group Size: Target 10 riders (maximum 15)
Estimated Duration: 22 March to 28 March 2027, including 5 riding days, one rest day and one travel day (for participants choosing the optional logistics package).
Point-to-point: each day will typically begin and end in a different location for riders participating in the planned daily routes.

3. Terrain & Riding Conditions
Participants should expect:
• Mixed terrain;
• Approximately 50% road / 50% off-road;
• Technical descents;
• Steep climbing;
• Hike-a-bike sections;
• Remote terrain;
• River crossings;
• Extreme heat exposure;
• Cold nights in elevated areas;
• Limited phone signal;
• Limited internet access.

4. Equipment Recommendations
Recommended equipment:
• Gravel/adventure bike;
• Low climbing gears;
• Reliable brakes and tyres;
• Tubeless setup strongly recommended;
• GPS navigation device strongly recommended;
• Spare tubes/tools;
• Layering for heat and cold;
• Front and rear lights where timing, weather, tunnels, road sections or low-light conditions require them;
• Helmet (mandatory).
E-bikes are not permitted.

5. Navigation & Communications
GPX files and route information will be provided before the event.
Participants are strongly encouraged to:
• Use a GPS device;
• Carry offline mapping;
• Obtain a suitable SIM/eSIM;
• Share live location where possible.
Phone signal may be absent for extended periods.

7. Safety & Emergency Considerations
Participants should understand:
• Medical facilities may be remote;
• Evacuation may be delayed;
• Conditions can change rapidly;
• Riders may become separated;
• Self-sufficiency is essential.

Hosts may provide informal guidance or assistance where possible but cannot guarantee support availability.
